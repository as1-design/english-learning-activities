🕵️ The Case of the Missing Actor

Leo Vance, Hollywood's golden boy, disappeared three days ago. As a private investigator, you are interviewing witnesses to trace his final steps. Analyze the clues carefully!

Clue 1: The Motive

You speak to Leo's bodyguard. "It started last Friday. Leo was paranoid and exhausted. He hates being followed by the paparazzi everywhere he goes, so he wanted to escape the city."

Who is doing the following in this sentence?

Clue 2: The Incident

You talk to the local police chief. "Later that night, Leo's Ferrari was found completely destroyed at the bottom of a cliff. He is believed to have crashed it while driving dangerously in the dark."

According to the police, when did the crash happen?

Clue 3: The Twist

Something doesn't add up. You track down Leo's ex-girlfriend. "I didn't believe the crash story, so I confronted him before he vanished. He finally admitted having lied about his whereabouts. He wasn't even in the car that night!"

What is the timeline of events?

Clue 4: The Discovery

Using his credit card records, you track him to a secret resort in Mexico. You show a photo to the hotel clerk. "Oh yes, that's the man in Cabana 4. Look at him over there. He seems to be enjoying a margarita by the pool!"

When is he enjoying his drink?

💼 Case Closed! The Full Story:

Brilliant detective work! You've pieced the story together: Leo was tired of the paparazzi tracking him (Passive). To escape, he paid someone else to destroy his car, creating a fake accident in the past (Perfect). His ex-girlfriend caught him, forcing him to confess to his earlier lies (Perfect). Now, while the world thinks he's missing, he's currently relaxing at a secret resort (Continuous).

Your Grammar Briefcase:

  • Passive Gerund: being + past participle (Actions done TO you)
  • Perfect Infinitive/Gerund: (to) have / having + past participle (Focuses on an action completed in the PAST)
  • Continuous Infinitive: to be + -ing (Actions happening right NOW)

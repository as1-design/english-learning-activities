What Lies Ahead —
A Grammar of the Future

Everything you need before tackling the activities. Open each section, study the examples, and keep this tab handy.

Fact / certain futureIt will be an expensive trip.
Opinion-based predictionI think they'll win the election.
Spontaneous decisionI'll get the bill — don't worry.
Promise / offer / refusalI promise I'll call you back.
First conditionalWe'll have fun if you come.
Think of will as a declaration — made in the moment, based on opinion or intention. If you decide as you speak, use will.
Intention / planI'm going to apply for a new job.
Evidence-based predictionLook at those clouds — it's going to rain!
Past unfulfilled planWe were going to move abroad, but then Brexit happened.
Intention (going to)I'm going to visit my mother this weekend.
Arrangement (present continuous)I'm visiting my mother this weekend.
The plan already exists in your head before you speak. If you can picture it as a mental to-do list item, use going to.
Arrangement (action taken)We're leaving early in the morning. (tickets booked)
Diary planShe's meeting the director on Friday.
Something is already in the diary. A step has been taken to confirm it. Present continuous = it's happening, full stop.
Timetabled eventThe train leaves at 7:42.
After time conjunctionsbefore / after / as soon as / until / whenCall me when you arrive.
After when, before, after, as soon as, until — never use will. The present simple takes over.
In progress at a future timeThis time next week, I'll be hiking in Menorca.
Polite enquiryWill you be attending the conference?
Inevitable / natural courseI'll be seeing Sarah at the party anyway.
Temporary situationYou'll be taking the train next week.
Picture a camera filming a future moment — the future continuous captures what's happening in frame. Also your most polite tool for asking about someone's arrangements.
be due to + inf.The report is due to be published on Friday.
be about to + inf.She's about to resign. (very soon)
be on the verge/brink of + -ingHe was on the brink of quitting.
be + to + inf. (formal)The president is to address the nation.
be bound toIt's bound to be a success. (very likely)
be (un)likely toShe's unlikely to accept the offer.
About to = seconds away. On the verge of = dramatic, significant. Due to = scheduled. Bound to = almost certain.
